The fake data project

Generated data on topics that are difficult to quantify.

Most nuanced topics have no dataset behind them. How relevant was democracy in 1994? How much did the world care about cultural history in 2007? No one was measuring, so there is nothing to cite.

This project uses AI to estimate those numbers. For every topic in the archive, an AI rates the topic’s relevance for each year from 1980 to 2020, on a scale from 0 to 1. The scores are stored, charted, and compared against the trends of other topics.

How it works

  1. A topic enters the archive.
  2. An AI scores the topic’s relevance for each year from 1980 to 2020, on a scale from 0 to 1.
  3. Topics are re-scored across multiple runs over time.
  4. The resulting series are charted and correlated against other topics’ trends.

None of this is measured data. Every figure is an estimate produced by AI, without sources or verification. Read it as a record of how an AI summarizes the world, not as fact.
